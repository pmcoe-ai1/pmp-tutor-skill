# PMP Exam Content Outline — summary (2021 edition, still in effect 2026)

Authoritative source: PMI's Exam Content Outline PDF at https://www.pmi.org/-/media/pmi/documents/public/pdf/certifications/pmp-examination-content-outline.pdf

This is a PMCOE-authored summary. For the binding source, always cite PMI's PDF directly.

## Exam structure

- 180 questions.
- 230 minutes.
- Two 10-minute breaks (one after Q60, one after Q120). Breaks are optional; the timer pauses.
- Question types: multiple choice (single answer), multiple response (select 2 or 3), matching, drag-and-drop, hot spot, limited fill-in-the-blank.
- Online proctored (your home / office) or in-person at a Pearson VUE test centre.

## Three domains

### Domain I — People (42%)

Tasks:
1. Manage conflict.
2. Lead a team.
3. Support team performance.
4. Empower team members and stakeholders.
5. Ensure team members / stakeholders are adequately trained.
6. Build a team.
7. Address and remove impediments, obstacles, and blockers for the team.
8. Negotiate project agreements.
9. Collaborate with stakeholders.
10. Build shared understanding.
11. Engage and support virtual teams.
12. Define team ground rules.
13. Mentor relevant stakeholders.
14. Promote team performance through the application of emotional intelligence.

### Domain II — Process (50%)

Tasks:
1. Execute project with the urgency required to deliver business value.
2. Manage communications.
3. Assess and manage risks.
4. Engage stakeholders.
5. Plan and manage budget and resources.
6. Plan and manage schedule.
7. Plan and manage quality of products / deliverables.
8. Plan and manage scope.
9. Integrate project planning activities.
10. Manage project changes.
11. Plan and manage procurement.
12. Manage project artefacts.
13. Determine appropriate project methodology / methods and practices.
14. Establish project governance structure.
15. Manage project issues.
16. Ensure knowledge transfer for project continuity.
17. Plan and manage project / phase closure or transitions.

### Domain III — Business Environment (8%)

Tasks:
1. Plan and manage project compliance.
2. Evaluate and deliver project benefits and value.
3. Evaluate and address external business environment changes for impact on scope.
4. Support organisational change.

## Lifecycle mix

About half the questions are predictive (traditional waterfall, fixed-scope, deliverable-focused). The other half are agile or hybrid. PMI does NOT publish an exact percentage; PMI's official wording is "approximately half." The common test-prep assumption of 50/50 is a reasonable working number; 60/40 or 40/60 in either direction is also defensible per recent exam-day reports.

## What the exam tests

- **Scenario interpretation**, not term-definition recall. The exam rarely asks "what is a stakeholder?" but very often gives a scenario and asks "what is the BEST next action?"
- **The most appropriate next step**, given several plausible options. The exam tests judgment — what a competent PM does *first*, *next*, or *most*.
- **PMI framework alignment**, not personal opinion. Even when multiple actions could work in real life, the exam wants the action most consistent with PMI's Talent Triangle and the values in the PMI Code of Ethics.

## Common student misconceptions

1. "If I memorise PMBOK, I'll pass." — No. PMBOK is reference material; the exam tests judgment.
2. "Agile is just Scrum." — No. The exam covers Scrum, Kanban, XP, Lean, and hybrid combinations.
3. "Earned Value is rare on the new exam." — Wrong. EV remains common, especially CPI / SPI / EAC formulas.
4. "If two answers seem right, pick the longer one." — No. Length is not a signal. Pick the answer most aligned with PMI's framework.
5. "I can skip the People domain if I'm experienced." — Wrong. People is 42% of the exam, the largest single domain.

## What's not on the exam

- Tooling questions (Asana, Jira, MS Project specifics).
- Non-PMI methodologies as competing approaches (PRINCE2 mechanics, SAFe ceremony specifics — these may appear contextually but aren't tested as PMI doctrine).
- Pure technical content (programming, accounting beyond cost-management basics, etc.).
