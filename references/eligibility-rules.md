# PMP Eligibility Rules

Authoritative source: PMI's PMP Handbook at https://www.pmi.org/-/media/pmi/documents/public/pdf/certifications/project-management-professional-handbook.pdf

## The two pathways

### Pathway A — with a four-year degree (bachelor's, global equivalent, or post-graduate)

- 36 months of leading-and-directing project experience within the past eight years.
- 35 contact hours of formal project management education.

### Pathway B — without a four-year degree (high school, associate's, or global equivalent)

- 60 months of leading-and-directing project experience within the past eight years.
- 35 contact hours of formal project management education.

## What counts as "leading and directing"

Per PMI's handbook: leading and directing involves projects with these characteristics:
- You manage at least one of: budget, scope, time, quality, resources, stakeholders, communications, risk, procurement, integration.
- The work is project-based (temporary, unique deliverable), not ongoing operational work.
- You are responsible for the outcome — not merely a team member contributing to someone else's project.

## What does NOT count

- Operational / repetitive work (running a customer support team is operations, not a project).
- Pure individual-contributor work where you didn't lead anything.
- Volunteering or unpaid internships, unless they meet the leading-and-directing definition with documentation.
- Overlapping months for parallel projects — if you led three projects in March 2024, that's one month, not three.

## Audit

PMI audits roughly 1 in 10 applications, randomly or on patterns that look suspicious (very round numbers, identical sponsor names across multiple projects, dates that don't add up).

Audit asks you to:
- Submit sponsor signoff for each project listed.
- Provide documentation of your 35 contact hours (course completion certificates).
- Possibly provide additional employer verification.

You have 90 days to respond to an audit. Failure to respond is treated as withdrawal.

## Contact hours

35 contact hours of formal education in project management. Sources that count:
- Authorised Training Partner courses (PMCOE's PMP bootcamp delivers 35+ contact hours).
- PMI chapter events.
- University courses on project management (each credit hour generally = 12-15 contact hours).
- Many online courses (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, etc.) with completion certificates.

Sources that don't:
- Self-study from books alone.
- Watching YouTube videos without an enrolled course structure.
- Operational training unrelated to project management.

## Eligibility audit advice

If a student asks "am I eligible?", walk through these questions:

1. "Do you have a four-year degree (bachelor's or global equivalent)?"
   - Yes → Pathway A → need 36 months.
   - No → Pathway B → need 60 months.

2. "Across the past eight years, can you account for at least {N} months of project work where you led at least one of budget / scope / schedule / quality / resources / stakeholders / risk?"
   - If yes → eligible for the experience requirement.
   - If unsure → walk through their actual job titles and project list.

3. "Have you completed 35 contact hours of formal project management education?"
   - Yes (with completion certificates) → eligible for the education requirement.
   - No → the next step is to enrol in a course (PMCOE's PMP bootcamp delivers this requirement).

4. If both pass → student is eligible. Encourage them to apply through certification.pmi.org and prepare for possible audit.

5. If one fails → identify the gap and suggest the specific remedy.

## When CAPM is better

If the student doesn't yet have the experience for PMP, CAPM may be a better near-term step:

- CAPM eligibility: secondary degree (high school diploma) plus 23 hours of project management education.
- No experience requirement.
- Often appropriate for: early-career professionals, career-pivoters, business/non-PM roles that interact with projects.

## What to tell the student honestly

- "PMI is strict about the 35 contact hours — those without a completion certificate from a formal source rarely make it through audit. If your contact hours are from informal sources, take a formal course before applying."
- "If you've never been audited, that's not a guarantee — audits are random. Document your project list and sponsor contacts before you apply, not after the audit email arrives."
- "If you're close on experience (say, 30 months when you need 36), wait. PMI's eligibility-clock is precise."
