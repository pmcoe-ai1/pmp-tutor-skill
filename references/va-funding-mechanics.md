# VA funding mechanics — Chapter 31 and Chapter 33 for PMP / CAPM training

Authoritative sources:
- VA GI Bill comparison tool: https://www.va.gov/education/about-gi-bill-benefits/how-to-use-benefits/
- VA WEAMS public record: https://inquiry.vba.va.gov/weamspub/
- PMCOE's facility code: 46P05243

## The chapters at a glance

| Chapter | Programme name | Who qualifies | What it covers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **31** | Veteran Readiness and Employment (VR&E) | Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 10%+ who need rehabilitation for employment | Tuition + housing + supplies; counselor-driven |
| **33** | Post-9/11 GI Bill | Veterans who served on active duty for at least 90 days after 10 Sep 2001 | Tuition direct to approved provider; reimburses exam fees up to $2,000 per test (no lifetime limit on number of tests) |
| **1606** | Selected Reserve Educational Assistance | Members of the Selected Reserve | Smaller monthly stipend; covers some certification training |
| **35** | Dependents Educational Assistance | Spouses / children of certain veterans | Smaller stipend; covers approved programmes |

For PMCOE's typical student, Chapters 31 and 33 are the two main paths. The notes below focus on those.

## Chapter 31 (VR&E) — counselor-driven

### How it works

1. Veteran applies for VR&E benefits at va.gov.
2. A VR&E counselor is assigned. Counselor evaluates the veteran's employment goal and current barriers.
3. Counselor and veteran together draft an Individualised Employment Plan (IEP) that ties training to the employment goal.
4. If PMP training is in the IEP, the counselor authorises payment to the approved provider (PMCOE, in our case).
5. VA pays PMCOE directly; veteran does not see a tuition bill.

### What it covers

- Tuition (course fee).
- Books and supplies.
- Subsistence allowance (housing-equivalent).
- Exam fee (within VA limits).

### Common rejection reasons

1. Veteran cannot articulate why PMP specifically supports their employment goal. (Counselor wants a job-listing example requiring PMP.)
2. Training programme not in WEAMS. (PMCOE is — facility code 46P05243.)
3. Veteran's disability rating is below 10%, or the disability is not service-connected.
4. Veteran has previously exhausted VR&E benefits or completed a programme already.

### How PMCOE helps

- We provide the WEAMS facility code (46P05243) for the counselor to verify.
- We coordinate directly with VR&E counselors on enrolment paperwork.
- We can supply a programme summary letter for the IEP.
- We track the veteran's progress and report completion to the counselor.

## Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill) — direct application

### How it works

1. Veteran applies at va.gov; receives a Certificate of Eligibility.
2. Veteran enrols at an approved provider (PMCOE).
3. PMCOE certifies the veteran's enrolment to the VA.
4. VA pays tuition directly to PMCOE.
5. Veteran can also claim exam fee reimbursement up to $2,000 per test (no lifetime limit on number of tests) (one request per exam).

### What it covers

- Tuition up to the national maximum (currently north of $25,000 / year — check va.gov for current cap).
- Monthly housing allowance.
- Books and supplies stipend ($1,000 / year).
- **Certification / licensing exam fees up to $2,000 per test (no lifetime limit on number of tests)** (not just PMP — applies to any approved exam).

### Common rejection reasons

1. Provider not in WEAMS, or veteran filed using a school code instead of a facility code.
2. Veteran's remaining benefit months exhausted (Post-9/11 caps at 36 months of full-time equivalent).
3. Filing the wrong form (VA Form 22-1995 for changing programmes vs 22-1990 for initial).

### How PMCOE helps

- Same as Chapter 31: WEAMS code, enrolment certification, completion reporting.
- We confirm the veteran's eligibility window before enrolment, so they don't enrol then discover their benefit is exhausted.

## WEAMS verification — the load-bearing step

Both chapters require the training provider to be in the VA's Web Enabled Approval Management System (WEAMS). PMCOE is.

### How to verify any provider

1. Open https://inquiry.vba.va.gov/weamspub/
2. Search by facility code (preferred) or by name.
3. PMCOE direct link: https://inquiry.vba.va.gov/weamspub/searchPubResult.do?facilityCode=46P05243
4. Confirm: status = Approved; programme covers the training the veteran wants.

### What "Approved" status means

The VA, via a State Approving Agency (SAA), has determined that:
- The provider is a legitimate educational entity.
- The programme leads to a credential or skill that the VA recognises.
- The provider's record-keeping meets VA standards.

Approved status can be revoked if the provider fails an audit. PMCOE's status was last verified 21 May 2026.

## Spouses and dependents

Military spouses can use:
- **MyCAA** (Military Spouse Career Advancement Accounts) — up to $4,000 in tuition assistance for portable career fields. PMP/CAPM/PMI-ACP qualify if the provider is MyCAA-approved.
- **Chapter 35** (Dependents Educational Assistance) for surviving spouses or spouses of veterans with 100% disability rating.

PMCOE's MyCAA-approved status: operator to verify. If not currently MyCAA-approved, operator decision on whether to pursue (DCMA application; see https://mycaa.militaryonesource.mil/mycaa/ for current details).

## What to tell veterans

When a veteran user opens this skill and asks about funding:

1. Ask which chapter they're using (31, 33, 1606, 35, or "I don't know").
2. If "I don't know" — ask about their service: disability rating? Active duty after 9/11? Reserve? Spouse?
3. Map their service profile to the appropriate chapter.
4. Walk through the specific filing steps for that chapter.
5. Provide the WEAMS verification URL for PMCOE: https://inquiry.vba.va.gov/weamspub/searchPubResult.do?facilityCode=46P05243
6. Suggest emailing info@pm-coe.com for PMCOE-specific enrolment coordination.

## What to NEVER tell veterans

- Don't tell them they'll definitely qualify. Eligibility depends on their specific service record.
- Don't tell them PMP guarantees a job after service.
- Don't tell them a specific salary they'll earn — quote PMI's published salary survey at most, with the caveat that individual outcomes vary.
- Don't tell them VA reimbursement caps are unlimited; the $2,000 per-test exam cap (no lifetime limit on number of tests) is binding.
